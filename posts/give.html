--- layout: post title: How would you like to be remembered? date: Oct 12, 2021 ---

A realization came to me as I sat in my mother-in-law's house on a stuffy Indian afternoon, listening to people eulogize my father-in-law.

He had passed away a few days before. My father-in-law came from humble beginnings. He couldn't complete his college because he had to start working to support his family. He took up a job as a welder making coaches for Indian Railways. Yet, with that small income, he found a way to raise two sons that ended up making great careers for themselves. He was very proud of his son's lives - our lives. But he was no slouch himself. He, alongside his wife, had managed to raise his family out of poverty.

But few of the folks that shared their memories of my father-in-law talked about his successes. None of them talked about his wealth or his work. They talked about his kindness. About how he had helped the flower seller who squatted outside his house when the flower seller most needed money. About how he had helped someone send his kids to college. About how he had helped someone else with their medical bills. About his compassion and his big heart.

I realized at that moment that what people remember most when you leave this world is not how much you worked or how much you earned, but how you touched the lives of others. Your legacy will likely not be your job titles.

This is why the month of October is very special to me. As folks in high tech, we have the kind of access of resources that few in the world have today. At the same time, there are so many causes crying out for our attention. I love that, at Microsoft, we put so much energy and passion to give back to the world during the Give Campaign month.
